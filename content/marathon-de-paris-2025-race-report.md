# Goals

It's always best to have multiple goals going into a race, just in case something goes wrong and the main goal becomes unreachable. So here were mine:

- Main goal was to maintain an average pace of 4'30"/km on the watch. The real/offical pace reported on the certificate after the race is always a few seconds slower. So 4'30" would translate to 4'33"/km real pace.
- Secondary goal was to finish under 3h15'.
- Last goal was to PR and beat my previous time (3h27').

If I'm honest, I knew before starting that my main goal was a little unrealistic. I was going to set that pace on my watch and try to hold it, despites the many ups and downs of the course. The total elevation of that marathon is nearing 300 meters, so very challenging.

My secondary goal had been my main goal for a while until I settled on a target pace of 4'30", which then became my main goal. Anyways... This meant a 12-minute improvement over my personal record, which was 3h27'. Quite significant!

# Training

I signed up for the Paris Marathon exactly a year ago, but I did not start training specifically for it before early January this year. After my first marathon in October last year, I took November off running and slowly eased back in over the month of December.

Once again, I created a 14-week training plan using the app "Kiprun Pacer" by Decathlon. I mostly stuck to it and hardly skipped any session. I only missed a few in the last week of January since I went skiing. I swapped the intervals and long runs with easy slow jogs.

I created a plan that comprised 4 sessions a week. Most weeks looked like this: one short easy jog, one run with marathon pace, one session with interval and one long run. The weeks were grouped in multiple blocks:

- first week was evalutation week, that finished with a MAS test
- then came the ramp up block
- then came the MAS block, aimed at improving my max speed/MAS. This block was 2, 3 or 4 week long, I forgot. It was concluded with another MAS test to assess improvement since the beginning of the training plan.
- then, marathon pace specific block. 4 week long I think it was.
- and finally, 3-week long taper

On top of the 4 sessions per week given by the app, I added a fifth session, every week. A short (< 60 mins) and slow (not faster than 5'20"/km) jog, ran with barefoot/minimalist shoes (Altra Escalante Racer 2), aimed at strengthening my legs and feet without risking getting injured.

So a typical week would look like:

- Monday: jog, sometimes with active blocks
- Wednesday: marathon pace
- Thursday: jog with barefoot shoes
- Friday: intervals
- Sunday: long run

On top of running, I was swimming on average once a week (between 1,000m and 2,000m), commuting 40 kms per week to work, and would add one bike tour on Saturdays from time to time. From March onwards, I also addedd one or two sessions of home trainer per week (< 45 mins each session). I had pretty full weeks!

February was a very challenging month with temperatures very often dropping under zeros, and almost 3 weeks of snow on the ground, which made it very slippery/icy!
